About This Site

I'm a master's student at TU Chemnitz, Germany, studying Design and Test for Integrated Circuits. This is my learning journal — published in public.

Everything here is built around one belief: circuits make sense when you visualize them. Not when you memorize formulas, not when you plug numbers into equations you don't understand — but when you feel why the current flows, why the voltage drops, why the transistor amplifies.

Why I'm Doing This

I come from an ECE background in India (LPU, 2020-2024 — COVID batch). My first two years were fully online. I know what it feels like to stare at a formula and wonder what it actually means.

My proudest achievement so far: building a motor controller with VGA display on an FPGA (Nexys 4 board) — completely self-researched, no AI assistance. When I explain things, I explain them the way I needed them explained.

Analog/Mixed-Signal IC Design. Companies like Infineon Dresden, Bosch Semiconductor, NXP, Texas Instruments. Long-term vision: build a semiconductor ecosystem in India.

But that's the destination. Right now, Chapter 2 of Sadiku is the mountain. One step at a time.
